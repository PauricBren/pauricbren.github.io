Engineering & Professional Experience

Tokyo Electron Ltd

Worked onsite at Intel in the etch service department for Tokyo Electron, supporting one of the largest & highest-throughput semiconductor tool groups. Operated in a high-pressure manufacturing environment performing real-time troubleshooting, repair and maintenance on complex systems. Collaborated with teams of 4 to 8 engineers to diagnose and resolve issues on electrical, pneumatic, mechanical, RF, vacuum, gas, firmware and software subsystems.

My responsibilities involved hands-on fault isolation, component replacement, system calibration, preventative maintenance and coordination with Intel teams. Other assigned tasks were documenting issue lifecycles, managing part orders and ensuring safe working practices through extensive safety training.

My troubleshooting duties required analysing & utilizing documentation, wiring diagrams, schematics, block diagrams & machine data such as logs, alarm/error codes & interlocks. 

Later in my role, I acted as shift lead cover by being a point of contact for customer communication, prioritising and coordinating tasks.

This position allowed me to develop strong skills in system-level thinking, hardware debugging, technical documentation and cross-disciplinary engineering. I have learned to understand a system as a whole and to spot failures across multiple domains.

Helgen Technologies Ltd

During my time at Helgen Technologies, I worked with a small multidisciplinary engineering team to develop an autonomous forklift robot from the ground up. My contribution to the project spanned across software, simulation and mechanical design. This gave me practical experience in the robotics development pipeline. 

My primary tasks involved refining & testing robot behaviours in Isaac Sim with ROS2. This task required programming modular nodes in C++ & Python, tuning navigation algorithms and building realistic simulation environments. Alongside my software contributions, I supported mechanical design with the use of Onshape CAD design. I also conducted in depth research into robotic components as to compile a comprehensive bill of materials for the first prototype. 

Throughout the internship, I worked within an agile based workflow by attending daily stand up meetings, code reviews and collaborating with my team to diagnose issues and improve system reliability. This experience strengthened my ability to work efficiently in fast-moving engineering environments while contributing meaningfully to a real-world robotics project. 

Johnson Controls

As part of my University work placement I got an opportunity to work with Johnson Controls. I operated among different teams to ensure delivery of several projects with the IT department using scrum and agile workflows. I attended daily meetings for team collaboration and developed thorough project requirements based on the end user's needs.

I was also given the opportunity to work alongside a software integration intern team to develop and test a chat bot solution among other projects to benefit the company greatly. 

This internship also offered me extensive training in a variety of business acumen as part of the internship program such as effective communication, organization health and professional network development. I also participated in an Internship challenge that involved working as part of a team to develop innovative solutions to relevant company issues.

project utilizing drones and Al for powerline management. I helped build a dataset and organized experiments to produce well defined recommendations to ESB staff moving forward with this project. We offered innovative solutions for optimizing the dataset and project deliverables. As part of the internship we also participated in a competition to propose a technical and business solution regarding green energy and heat pumps for the company.
